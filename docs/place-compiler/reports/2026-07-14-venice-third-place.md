# Venice third-place proof

Venice Historic Core is the third accepted Place Compiler artifact and the first proof outside the United States. It uses the same recipe, immutable generation, profile, inspection, ecology, performance, route, sightline, atlas, packaging, and verification machinery as San Francisco and Lower Manhattan. No Venice-specific implementation branch was added.

## Why Venice

The 3,004×2,113-block core stresses a different structure: 3.8 m of real relief, dense irregular pedestrian fabric, canals, many small bridges, water-sensitive landmarks, and global Mapterhorn terrain instead of USGS 3DEP. Its frozen recursive OSM snapshot contains 529,794 elements in 58,728,711 bytes and is bound to the exact Overpass query and payload digest.

Generation completed in 21 seconds with 894 MB maximum resident memory. The immutable world contains 42 files, 30 Anvil regions, and 210,184,393 bytes with tree digest `5f88058850389cc7aed5fe59e52db8b4af510683b1389b8c3c3ffcce4934b56b`.

## Evidence-driven repair

The initial Campo San Polo arrival was geometrically valid but died 30 times during one accelerated native day. A second natural candidate on the generated Rialto Bridge survived, so the experience policy now declares Rialto rather than weakening Minecraft ecology or adding protection blocks.

The original Arsenal point correctly landed inside its enclosed basin and therefore failed a ground-arrival assertion. The experience policy moves the acceptance marker to the Porta Magna pedestrian approach, which inspected as built `polished_diorite_stairs` with headroom. The recipe and immutable world remain unchanged.

## Accepted measurements

- Rialto advanced 24,014 native ticks with zero deaths and clean shutdown at 406 effective TPS. Mob spawning, daylight, weather, and random ticks remained Minecraft-authoritative.
- The quality verifier reports `accepted-with-frontiers`; all six acceptance checks are green, including the Arsenal checkpoint correction.
- Two repetitions of every profile were stable. Cinematic measured 2,579–2,831 TPS, playable 386–389 TPS, and living 399–408 TPS. Minimum realtime headroom was 19.3×.
- Five landmark fields had complete column coverage. Campo San Polo, Rialto, Santa Lucia, St Mark's Square, and the corrected Arsenal approach all expose built representative ground.
- Sightline evidence shows the low-rise city form: Rialto-to-Arsenal clears with a 32-block lift; St Mark's-to-Rialto requires a 256-block reveal because nearby generated fabric blocks the base ray.
- The frozen pedestrian route from Santa Lucia through Campo San Polo and Rialto to St Mark's resolves 95.28% of sparse samples, but only 76.30% of its swept path is continuously supported and collision-free in the generated world.

## Honest frontiers

- The route result is not accepted as a clean human ground tour. Venice exposes that a finite Arnis road-palette whitelist and external pedestrian centerline are insufficient in narrow, vertically varied historic fabric. The evidence is retained for a generic walkable-surface/continuity repair.
- The Rialto sample ends with 38 of 42 visible entities hostile, and five sparse checkpoints expose only two biome IDs. These remain bounded ecology/experience candidates, not world-wide claims.
- Sightline endpoints use local generated peaks and measure physical voxels, not client LOD or scenic camera quality.
- The first human interventions were limited to choosing reviewable bounds and landmarks, rejecting unsafe Campo from real evidence, and correcting the Arsenal's semantic role from basin center to pedestrian approach.
