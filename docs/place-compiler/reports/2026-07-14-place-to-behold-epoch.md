## Real consequence and restart

Epoch one spawned `ProofResident` on the audited Rialto surface. The resident observed one dropped apple, navigated through the normal safety and action interpreter, and received Minecraft's `playerCollect` confirmation. A fresh `ProofWitness` connection then observed no dropped apple. The managed owner saved the world, stopped the controller and server, closed its chained lifecycle journal, and released control.

Epoch two acquired a new owner epoch over the same runtime. Minecraft restored the resident's changed position and apple inventory. Behold restored one prior authoritative loom turn, performed an inspection rather than repeating collection, and appended turn two to the same loom. The second owner also saved, stopped, journaled, and released cleanly.

All nine report assertions pass. The act and resume lifecycle tips are `c980aa…274` and `8be0bf…51d`; the authoritative loom digest is `ea051b…a1cb`. Runtime trees changed after the first consequence and again after the second clean save, while source and baseline identities remained fixed.

The first clean attempt is retained only as diagnostic history: it placed the affordance two blocks east of Rialto's verified arrival column, where it fell over unsupported canal air. The resident correctly refused `unsafe_item_destination`. The successful run used two independently inspected supported bridge columns; no world block or safety rule was patched to force success.
