# Turning world research into stored capability

We are building an inhabitation layer. A mind receives what one body can
perceive, chooses from what that body can attempt, or waits. The world decides
what actually happens. The resulting experience returns to the mind through a
continuing personal life.

That life is not a log we keep after the intelligence has finished. It is part
of where the intelligence lives. A new model process can rehydrate from it in
roughly the way a new inference process rehydrates from message history after
its transient internal state is gone.

We are learning how to build this by attaching minds to several very different
worlds. Those are research channels, not pieces that must all be folded into
one framework.

## The yield rule

A research channel has returned useful energy when it produces at least one of
these:

- a small primitive that repeats cleanly in another world;
- an evidence asset that reliably catches a false claim;
- a demo that lets another person feel or understand a new capability.

An idea does not deserve a package merely because it is interesting. We should
extract a package after two independent uses agree on a narrow, deep boundary.
Until then, keep it local, named, tested, and easy to replace.

The Place Compiler, Golarion, World Instrument, ALMO, Cantrip, and Armok Lab
repositories were reread at their current local heads before writing this map.
They remain independent products and experiments. The value comes from shared
lessons and deliberately tiny junctions, not shared internal machinery.

## What Behold has earned

Behold has earned one clear elevator pitch:

> Attach a replaceable mind to a persistent body in a real world, let it live
> through the world's native affordances and authority, and preserve enough
> private causal history to resume and verify its life.

It has also earned three implementation boundaries inside the repository:

1. The inhabitation runtime owns admission, body lease, observation delivery,
   mind invocation, action arbitration, terminal delivery, and safe shutdown.
2. The life substrate owns one inhabitant's durable private trajectory and
   exact episode references. Lync implements the immutable center.
3. The evaluation boundary can reconstruct an inhabited turn into distinct
   observation, proposal, decision, execution, world fact when earned, and
   outside check records.

The third boundary is now live and independently reassessed twice in Minecraft:
one uncoached Ax action correctly emits no material fact, while one scripted
native placement earns an air-to-dirt fact only after an exact Minecraft block
update and a post-quiescence fresh-body read. This proves the inhabitation and
verification composition, not the scripted resident's intelligence. The module
should remain internal until a second world conforms without Minecraft concepts
leaking into it.

## The offerings this can become

The same research can produce several clean offerings without forcing them
into one product.

- An inhabited-world runtime for people who already have a world and want to
  attach persistent, inspectable minds to its native bodies.
- A Minecraft experience and benchmark where humans and models receive roughly
  the same culturally intelligible player affordances.
- A life and episode layer for replay, evaluation, training data, handoff, and
  model replacement without copying private experience into public metrics.
- A verification kit that distinguishes what a mind saw, proposed, was allowed
  to attempt, actually executed, changed in the world, and had checked later.
- Joint demonstrations where a generated place becomes a living world and an
  inhabitant enters it, acts normally, and leaves persistent consequences.

The Minecraft adapter and its demos can be useful before the shared runtime is
ready as an external SDK. The shared runtime becomes credible after the second
world proof. Population scheduling and forked-world operations are later
products with different failure modes; they should not be smuggled into the
first package.

## The next big swings

The material-consequence checkpoint has now passed in a real Minecraft 1.21.4
epoch and survived independent reassessment. The next checkpoints should each
falsify a larger claim.

1. **Contrasting world.** Project one real Golarion turn through the same six
   causal distinctions. Keep its gauges, bodies, and authority local. If the
   graph bends, fix the graph before extracting it.
2. **Continuing intelligence.** Stop the controller, change or replace the mind
   program, resume the same body and life, and show that it remembers a real
   unfinished concern without repeating completed physical work.
3. **Two private lives.** Put two inhabitants in one epoch. Let one cause an
   ordinary world consequence the other later observes, without sharing
   private prompts, memories, inventories, or evaluator state.
4. **Optimization that matters.** Use exact Lync episode references and Ax
   program identities to optimize on training episodes, then require a cheaper
   or more capable held-out real rollout. Prompt changes receive no credit by
   themselves.
5. **Population frontier.** Measure latency, throughput, cost, storage growth,
   fairness, and active-region load as inhabitants are added. Intelligence can
   be upgraded or downgraded, but identity and life cannot silently change.
6. **World histories.** Join a Behold epoch to an immutable world checkpoint,
   fork two writable children, and preserve which life experienced which
   history. Keep world forks, controller forks, and evaluation branches
   separate.

The first two swings tell us whether the current abstraction is real. The next
two make continuing and multiplayer life tangible. The last three turn the
system into a learnable, scalable research and product platform.

## What not to build yet

Do not build a universal ecology, a universal affordance ontology, a generic
`fork()` operation, a prompt-heavy personality harness, or a standalone action
record package. Minecraft and each other world remain authoritative for their
own mechanics. Mind libraries remain replaceable. Shared code must be earned
by repeated use.
