# Minds can change while a life and world history continue

## Outcome

Behold now has one clean live composition of four boundaries that had previously
been proved separately:

- one stopped Minecraft save became an immutable checkpoint;
- two writable world histories began from that exact checkpoint;
- direct and Ax minds each made an untasked decision through the same resident
  contract in separate sibling histories;
- one Ax-started life then continued for two more epochs under the direct mind,
  without copying its Lync or resetting its world history.

The result is deliberately narrower than a competence benchmark. The resident
did not receive a task, required action, action allowlist, or forced provider
tool choice. Its useful action, failed no-op, and later correction are all part
of the evidence. A camera-orientation result is an embodied world-action fact,
not a claim that Minecraft terrain materially changed.

## Exact-input differential

The immutable request captured from the direct live turn had SHA-256
`314d3f6e79c1012a05234104fdae4d5a5d752653e86eeb533df88a2da4552d96`.
The mutation-disabled comparison harness gave that exact artifact to both
adapters. Both calls authenticated the same input and returned schema-valid,
admitted decisions. Direct proposed facing the oak log; Ax explicitly yielded
to gather more evidence.

That disagreement is useful. It proves the contract admits policy variation
instead of encoding one benchmark answer. The exact-input differential isolates
the mind but cannot prove a world consequence; the sibling rollouts prove real
consequences but cannot freeze native time. Together they control the two
different questions honestly.

## Repeated exact-request sampling

A later request from turn 3, after the life had experienced both the successful
orientation and the rejected no-op, was captured with SHA-256
`3f6ac68d61ebf4a43c86a9c9e453af858cc92efd2cbf008484e462bbdf0b056e`.
The mutation-disabled evaluator then sampled each adapter three times from that
one immutable request. All six calls completed, authenticated the exact input,
and returned schema-valid decisions.

| Mind   | Proposal distribution                                     | Tokens |       Cost | p50 / p95 latency |
| ------ | --------------------------------------------------------- | -----: | ---------: | ----------------: |
| Direct | face exact oak log ×2; no action ×1                       | 14,557 | $0.0024012 |   1.879 / 3.091 s |
| Ax 23  | face exact oak log ×2; face exact visible birch leaves ×1 | 23,549 | $0.0030632 |   2.878 / 3.154 s |

This run caught and removed three adapter artifacts before becoming evidence.
Provider-generated call IDs had incorrectly split identical direct actions into
separate histogram buckets. Ax also required a public utterance even though the
shared mind contract permits silent embodied action; that accidental output
requirement caused a real validation failure. Semantic action grouping, optional
utterance, and canonical `wait_for_event` decisions now pass the full gate. No
prompt, demonstration, action requirement, or world special case was added.

The distribution is not an outcome score. None of these replayed proposals was
executed, and three samples cannot rank policy quality. It does establish the
reusable measurement boundary needed next: adapter reliability, stochastic
proposal behavior, latency, tokens, cost, and provider attempts can be measured
without mutating the life or world. Outcome quality still requires separately
executed, independently witnessed, lineage-safe episodes.

## One life across three epochs and two minds

The Ax branch then continued rather than being reset. The proof runner first
reassessed the preceding episode, checked that the current branch digest was
exactly its recorded post-turn digest, and required the resident Lync to report
the authenticated prior turn count. An existing isolated server profile is now
reused only after its manifest and prepared files verify; a new profile still
cannot be created after a history has diverged.

All three life turns use Lync
`lync:019f633b-bb94-7d14-b8f8-8e6fdb19f57d`:

1. In epoch 1, Ax faced the oak log successfully.
2. In epoch 2, the direct mind received turn 1 as its own recent lived
   continuity and chose `look_direction {horizontal: same, vertical: same}`.
   The body correctly returned `look_direction_unchanged`; the failure and
   fresh observation became life turn 2.
3. In epoch 3, the same direct mind received both prior turns, including the
   exact failure, changed strategy, and faced the oak log successfully. No
   controller rule told it to repair the failure or select that target.

Each turn has a separate evaluator-owned Lync episode containing only a
reference to its exact one-turn resident range. After turn 3 appended, all
three episode ranges still passed clean-process reassessment. This establishes
that the continuing person is not identical to a server process, runtime epoch,
or mind adapter.

## What this does not prove

- It does not prove long-life Minecraft competence, survival, construction, or
  social behavior.
- It does not show that Ax is better or worse than direct. One request is not an
  optimization dataset.
- It does not claim a material terrain change from camera orientation.
- It does not yet provide train/held-out episode splits or a held-out optimized
  Ax program.
- It does not prove crossing one continuing private life into another world
  history or authorizing independently active copies.
- It does not establish population-scale cost or latency.

The next learning claim should use several lineage-safe episodes and reserve
whole checkpoint families for held-out evaluation. The controller contract,
world affordances, safety policy, and evaluator must remain fixed while an Ax
program or model changes.
